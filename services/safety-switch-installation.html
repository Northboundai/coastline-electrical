Safety switch installation

Safety switches cut the power in milliseconds if something goes wrong. If your home doesn't have them, this is the upgrade to make.

Book this service (02) 4321 1234

About this service

A safety switch, or RCD, monitors the flow of electricity and shuts it off in a fraction of a second if it detects a fault, like current leaking to earth through a person. It's the difference between a scare and a serious injury.

Plenty of older Central Coast homes still have no safety switches, or only one covering part of the house. We install RCDs to protect your power and lighting circuits as required under NSW wiring rules.

What's included

  • Check of your existing switchboard and protection
  • Supply and install of RCD safety switches
  • Coverage of power and lighting circuits as required
  • Testing of every safety switch
  • Advice if a full switchboard upgrade is the better option
  • Compliance certificate on completion

Safety switch installation FAQs

How do I know if I already have safety switches?

Look in your switchboard for switches with a 'T' or 'Test' button. If you can't see any, or aren't sure, send us a photo and we'll tell you.

Can you add RCDs to my old board?

Often yes, but if the board is very old it can be more cost-effective to upgrade the whole switchboard. We'll give you honest advice.

How often should I test my safety switches?

Press the test button every three months. If one doesn't trip, it needs attention, give us a call.

Are safety switches a legal requirement?

RCD protection is required on power and lighting circuits under NSW wiring rules, and on property sales and certain works.
