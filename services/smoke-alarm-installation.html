Smoke alarm installation

Working smoke alarms save lives. We supply, install and interconnect photoelectric alarms to meet NSW requirements.

Book this service (02) 4321 1234

About this service

Smoke alarms are the cheapest life-saving device you can install, and the rules around them are stricter than most people realise, especially for rental properties.

We install photoelectric smoke alarms in the right locations, interconnect them so they all sound together, and make sure your home or rental meets current NSW requirements.

What's included

  • Assessment of alarm locations against NSW requirements
  • Supply and install of photoelectric smoke alarms
  • Interconnection so all alarms sound together
  • Testing of every alarm
  • Compliance documentation for rentals
  • Removal of old or expired alarms
Book this service

Smoke alarm jobs get an upfront fixed price based on the number of alarms and whether they're hard-wired or long-life.

Why book it with us

Photoelectric alarms

Photoelectric alarms detect smouldering fires faster and have fewer nuisance alarms than older ionisation types. They're the recommended standard.

Interconnected for safety

Interconnected alarms all sound at once, so you're woken no matter which end of the house the smoke starts in.

Rental compliance

We keep landlords and agents compliant with NSW smoke alarm laws, with certificates for your records.

Hard-wired or long-life

We install mains-powered alarms with battery backup, or long-life lithium alarms where hard-wiring isn't practical.

Smoke alarm installation FAQs

How many smoke alarms do I need?

It depends on your home's layout, but NSW requires alarms on every storey and near sleeping areas. We assess and advise at the booking.

Do rentals have stricter rules?

Yes. There are specific requirements for rental properties in NSW, and we make sure your property complies and provide documentation.

How often should alarms be replaced?

Smoke alarms have a use-by date, usually around 10 years. We'll flag any that are expired and should be replaced.

Hard-wired or battery?

We recommend mains-powered alarms with battery backup where wiring allows, or long-life sealed lithium alarms otherwise.
