Choosing LED lighting for your home

Coastline Electrical·16 April 2026·5 min read

Switching to LED lighting is one of the best-value upgrades you can make, lower bills, less heat, and globes that last for years. But there's more to a good result than just buying the cheapest downlights. Here's how to get it right.

Colour temperature: warm vs cool

Colour temperature is measured in kelvin (K). Warm white (around 2700-3000K) feels cosy and suits living areas and bedrooms. Cool white (around 4000K) is crisper and works well in kitchens, bathrooms, laundries and garages. Daylight (5000K+) is usually too clinical for a home. Many people mix warm in living spaces and cool in work areas.

Brightness is about lumens, not watts

With LEDs, watts tell you energy use, not brightness, lumens do. A 9W LED downlight can put out as much light as an old 60W halogen. Think about how much light a room needs and how many fittings, rather than chasing a wattage number.

Dimming: get the combination right

If you want to dim, the LED, the driver and the dimmer all need to be compatible. The classic mistake is fitting LEDs to an old dimmer designed for halogens, which causes flickering, buzzing or a limited dimming range. We match dimmable LEDs to the right dimmer so it works smoothly.

Why LEDs flicker (and how to avoid it)

Flicker almost always comes down to a mismatch, a cheap driver, an incompatible dimmer, or too few fittings on a dimmer's minimum load. Quality fittings, the right driver and a compatible dimmer fix it. It's worth getting this right; flicker is annoying and can cause headaches.

Don't forget the layers

Good lighting uses layers: general (downlights), task (over benches and desks) and accent (pendants, strip lighting). A kitchen with downlights plus pendants over the island and strips under the cabinets feels far better than a grid of downlights alone.

Outdoor and sensor lighting

For outside, choose weather-rated fittings and consider sensor lights for security and convenience. On the Coast, look for corrosion-resistant fittings if they're exposed to salt air.

Get a tidy, flicker-free result
