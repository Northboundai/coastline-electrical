7 signs your switchboard needs upgrading

Coastline Electrical·28 May 2026·5 min read

Your switchboard quietly does one of the most important jobs in your home: distributing power safely and cutting it off when something goes wrong. On the Central Coast, plenty of switchboards are decades old and were never designed for the way we use electricity today. Here are the signs yours is due for an upgrade.

1. You still have ceramic fuses

If your board has little porcelain fuse holders with fuse wire inside, it's old technology. Rewireable fuses don't protect people the way modern safety switches do, and rewiring a blown fuse in the dark is both annoying and risky. Modern circuit breakers and RCDs are far safer and more convenient.

2. There are no safety switches (RCDs)

Safety switches cut the power in milliseconds if current leaks to earth, the kind of fault that can electrocute someone. If you can't find any switches with a 'Test' button in your board, you likely don't have RCD protection, which is required on power and lighting circuits in NSW. This is the single most important reason to upgrade.

3. The board trips constantly

The occasional trip is the system doing its job. But if your board trips whenever you run a few appliances together, it's a sign the board is overloaded or circuits are poorly arranged for modern loads like air-conditioning, induction cooktops and EV chargers.

4. It's hot, buzzing or scorched

A switchboard should be cool and quiet. Heat, buzzing, a burning smell or scorch marks point to loose connections or overloaded circuits, which are genuine fire risks. If you notice these, book a diagnostic visit, and if it's smoking, switch off the main if safe and call 000.

5. You're renovating or adding load

Adding air-conditioning, a pool, solar or an EV charger puts new demand on your board. A renovation is the ideal time to upgrade, while access is easy, and to leave room for the circuits you'll need.

6. There's no room for more circuits

A full board with no spare ways means every addition becomes a bigger job. Upgrading gives you capacity for the things you'll inevitably want to add later.

7. The home is 25+ years old and never upgraded

If your Coast home is a few decades old and the board has never been touched, it's worth having a licensed electrician take a look. Many older boards are safe enough day-to-day but well short of current safety standards.

What an upgrade involves

A switchboard upgrade is usually a one-day job. We assess your board and wiring, install a modern compliant board with RCD safety switches, label everything clearly, test each circuit and issue a compliance certificate. Power is only off for part of the day, and we plan it around you.

If a few of these signs sound familiar, book a site inspection and we'll give you a fixed, upfront quote, no obligation.
