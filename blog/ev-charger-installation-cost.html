How much does EV charger installation cost?

Coastline Electrical·14 May 2026·6 min read

It's the question every new EV owner asks, and the honest answer is: it depends. A straightforward install and a tricky one can differ by a lot, and a quote given sight-unseen is often wrong. Here's what actually drives the cost of a home charger on the Central Coast.

The charger itself

Chargers range from basic units to smart, app-connected models with load management and solar awareness. A good 7kW single-phase home charger is the sweet spot for most households. Three-phase chargers cost more but charge faster if your home has three-phase supply.

The cable run

The distance from your switchboard to where the charger will sit is one of the biggest cost factors. A charger on the garage wall right next to the board is cheap to run. A charger at the far end of a long driveway, or one that needs cable run underground or through a wall cavity, takes more labour and materials.

Your switchboard and supply

Older boards sometimes need work to add a safe, dedicated circuit for the charger, and occasionally a switchboard upgrade makes sense. We assess your supply and board at the inspection so the quote reflects what your home actually needs, rather than a guess.

Load management

If your supply is tight, smart load management lets the charger ease off when the house is drawing heavily, avoiding nuisance trips and the cost of a supply upgrade. It's often cheaper to add load management than to increase your supply.

Solar setup

If you have solar and want to charge off your own power, some chargers support solar-aware charging. Setting this up adds a little to the install but can pay for itself over time.

Why we inspect first

Because all of the above varies house to house, we quote EV chargers after a quick site inspection. You get a fixed written price that covers the charger, the circuit and any board work, so there are no nasty surprises on the day. It also means we can recommend the right charger rather than overselling you one your home can't fully use.

The bottom line

A typical Central Coast home charger install is a few hours' work for a licensed electrician once everything's agreed. The best way to get an accurate number is to book a site inspection. We'll assess your setup, recommend a charger, and give you a fixed quote with no obligation.
